# Resume Design Guide

## Direction

Clean developer resume, not a portfolio landing page. Prioritize readability, strong evidence, and fast scanning. The design should feel like a polished hiring document, not a decorative personal website.

## Visual Principles

- Use a single light document surface with generous margins.
- Keep the first viewport focused on name, role, positioning, contact, and four compact strength cards.
- Use cards only for top-level strengths. Experience content should remain text-first and scannable.
- Avoid sidebars, decorative gradients, illustrations, and heavy shadows.
- Avoid publishing unnecessary links. Keep GitHub and email only.

## Color Tokens

- Ink: `#171717`
- Canvas: `#f5f7fb`
- Surface: `#ffffff`
- Accent: `#2563eb`
- Accent Soft: `#eff6ff`
- Border: `#e6e8ec`

## Typography

- Font: Inter + Noto Sans KR
- Name: 34px desktop, 26px mobile
- Body: 15px, line-height 1.7
- Section label: 12px uppercase, letter spaced
- Avoid oversized hero typography. This is a resume, not a marketing page.

## Components

- Header: horizontal profile block, concise contact links, short positioning paragraphs.
- Strength pills: small, bordered, Korean labels.
- Highlight cards: 8px radius, subtle border, no heavy decoration.
- Experience items: company/title/date header, evidence-first bullets, clear section spacing.
- Skill section: four compact groups only.

## Content Rules

- Lead with problem, judgment, action, result.
- Use technology names as evidence, not the main sentence.
- Remove TODO, coaching notes, and uncertain claims from public files.
- Keep sensitive company internals abstracted.
